Nature of Chengdu

Chengdu is home to 80% of the world's giant pandas. The city is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, including the famous Jiuzhaigou Valley and Mount Qingcheng. The mild climate and abundant rainfall make Chengdu one of the most ecologically diverse regions in China.

Culture of Chengdu

Chengdu is known for its relaxed lifestyle and tea culture. The city has over 2000 years of history and is the birthplace of Sichuan opera with its famous face-changing performances. Chengdu people are known for their hospitality and love for leisure activities, often spending hours in tea houses playing mahjong or chatting with friends.

Chengdu Cuisine

Chengdu is a UNESCO City of Gastronomy, famous for its spicy Sichuan cuisine. The city is the birthplace of hot pot and mapo tofu, with flavors that are both numbing and spicy. Sichuan peppercorns create the unique 'mala' sensation that defines this regional cuisine.
